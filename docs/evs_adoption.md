**Scope:** EU27 Battery Electric Vehicles (BEV) — excludes UK and Norway  
**Period:** 2016–2025

### Notable Inflection Points

- **2020 spike (+172%):** Driven by expanded German "Umweltbonus" (up to €9,000 subsidy), French bonus écologique, and EU 95g/km fleet CO2 targets coming into force. Automakers aggressively registered EVs to avoid penalties.
- **2022 slowdown (+24%):** Semiconductor shortages constrained supply despite strong demand.
- **2023 record (+37%):** Fleet electrification accelerated; best year on record.
- **2024 contraction (-13%):** Germany abruptly terminated subsidies in November 2023, causing significant demand collapse. Market uncertainty spread to other member states.

## Estimated Fleet in Circulation (End of 2025)

The EU BEV fleet is very young (median age ~3–4 years as of 2025), so scrappage rates are negligible. The EU average vehicle lifetime is 18–21 years; annual scrappage for vehicles under 10 years old is estimated at <1–2%.

### Key Finding

> **Approximately 6.8 million BEVs are currently in circulation in the EU27** (end of 2025).

This represents roughly **2% of the total EU passenger car fleet** (~280 million vehicles). This estimate is consistent with EAFO stock figures, which put the EU BEV stock at 6.5–7.0 million by end-2025.

## Battery Lifespan and Scrappage Notes

- EU regulatory minimum battery warranty: 8 years / 160,000 km (varies by manufacturer).
- Median battery capacity loss: ~2.3% per year (Geotab/Recurrent studies) — most retain >80% capacity after 8–10 years.
- First-generation EVs (pre-2015 Nissan Leaf with air-cooled battery) degraded faster, but these are a small fraction of the EU fleet.
- Scrappage rates for BEVs are not yet well-established empirically due to the young fleet; figures above use standard ICE survival curves adjusted conservatively downward.
