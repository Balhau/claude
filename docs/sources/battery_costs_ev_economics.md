## Battery Pack Cost Trajectory ($/kWh)

| Year | Pack Cost ($/kWh) | Key Driver |
|------|-------------------|------------|
| 2016 | ~$273 | Early production scale |
| 2018 | ~$176 | Scale + chemistry improvements (NMC) |
| 2020 | ~$137 | LFP adoption begins |
| 2022 | ~$151 | Raw material spike (lithium, cobalt, nickel) |
| 2023 | ~$139 | Normalisation; LFP dominant in mass market |
| 2024 | ~$115 | Chinese manufacturing scale + LFP |
| 2025 | ~$107–115 | Continued LFP / sodium-ion emergence |
| 2027 (est.) | ~$85–95 | Solid-state pilots; scale |
| 2030 (est.) | ~$75–85 | Projected cost parity threshold |

*Source: BloombergNEF BNEF EVO 2024; IEA Global EV Outlook 2024*

## Cost Parity Analysis

**Sticker price parity** (BEV purchase price = equivalent ICE) is estimated to occur when battery packs reach ~$75–85/kWh:

| Segment | ICE Reference Price | Est. BEV Parity Year | Notes |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|-------|
| Small car (B-segment) | ~€18,000 | 2029–2031 | Hardest segment; small battery matters most |
| Compact/family (C-segment) | ~€26,000 | 2027–2029 | Largest EU volume segment |
| SUV/crossover (D-segment) | ~€35,000 | 2026–2028 | Already near parity in premium tier |
| Premium/luxury | ~€55,000+ | Reached ~2023 | Tesla already demonstrated |

**Total cost of ownership (TCO) parity** (including fuel savings) is already reached in most EU segments at current prices for high-mileage users.

## Battery Lifespan & Degradation Data

### Capacity Retention Over Time

Based on Geotab fleet telematics data (>6,000 EVs tracked):

| Vehicle Age | Median Capacity Retention |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| 1 year | ~98% |
| 3 years | ~95% |
| 5 years | ~92% |
| 8 years | ~85% |
| 10 years | ~80% |
| 12 years | ~75% |

- Annual degradation rate: **~2.3% median** (varies by chemistry, climate, charging habits)
- LFP batteries (increasingly dominant): degrade ~30–40% slower than NMC in real-world use
- Rapid (DC fast) charging accelerates degradation by ~0.1–0.3% extra per year at high use

### EU Regulatory Minimum Battery Warranty

- **8 years or 160,000 km**, retaining minimum 70% capacity (from 2024 for new models under EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542)

## Raw Material Dependency

| Material | Primary Use | Key Concern | EU Exposure |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Lithium | All battery chemistries | Supply concentration (Chile, Australia, China) | High import dependency |
| Cobalt | NMC batteries | DRC supply + ESG concerns | High; declining as LFP grows |
| Nickel | NMC high-energy | Russia supply historically significant | Moderate |
| Graphite | Anode | China dominates processing (~90%) | High |
| Manganese | LFP / LMFP | More geographically distributed | Moderate |

*The EU Critical Raw Materials Act (2024) targets 40% domestic processing capacity by 2030 to reduce dependency.*
