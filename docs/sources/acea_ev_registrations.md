## Key Context Notes

- **2020 spike:** German Umweltbonus subsidy expanded (up to €9,000); French bonus écologique; EU 95g/km CO2 fleet targets came into force — manufacturers registered EVs aggressively to avoid penalties.
- **2022 slowdown:** Global semiconductor shortage constrained vehicle production despite strong order books.
- **2024 contraction:** Germany terminated the Umweltbonus in November 2023 (budget ruling); shock withdrawal removed the EU's largest national EV subsidy programme.
- Pre-2020 figures have lower source confidence in EU27-only breakdowns; some ACEA series historically included Norway or UK.

## How to Verify

1. Go to https://www.acea.auto/statistics/
2. Select "Registrations" > "Passenger cars" > "By fuel type"
3. Download the annual summary Excel/PDF for each year
4. Filter for "Battery Electric" and EU27 aggregate
