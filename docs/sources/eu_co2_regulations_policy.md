## Key EU Legislation

### Regulation (EU) 2019/631 — CO2 Standards for Passenger Cars
- Sets fleet-average CO2 emission targets for new passenger cars sold in the EU
- Applies to car manufacturers based on their annual EU sales volumes

### Regulation (EU) 2023/851 — Amendment (2035 ICE Ban)
- Mandates **100% zero-emission new car sales from 2035**
- Includes a review clause in 2026 (political pressure to revise, outcome uncertain as of early 2025)

## CO2 Fleet Targets Timeline

| Year | CO2 Target (g/km, WLTP) | Reduction vs 2021 Baseline (~122 g/km) |
|------|--------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| 2020 | 95 g/km (NEDC) ≈ 118 g/km WLTP | — (transition year) |
| 2025 | 93.6 g/km (WLTP) | ~23% |
| 2030 | 49.5 g/km (WLTP) | ~55% |
| 2035 | 0 g/km | ~100% (zero-emission only) |

*WLTP = Worldwide Harmonised Light Vehicle Test Procedure (replaced NEDC in 2021)*

---

## Implied BEV Sales Share Required to Meet Targets

To achieve the 2030 target of ~49.5g/km with a mixed fleet:

| Assumption | Required BEV share of new sales by 2030 |
|------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Optimistic (PHEVs count fully, ICE at 100g avg.) | ~35% |
| Central (PHEVs at 50% credit, ICE at 110g avg.) | ~45% |
| Conservative (PHEVs phased out, strict ICE avg.) | ~55% |

*This is the primary regulatory driver behind the base-case forecast of ~3.2M BEV sales (32% share) by 2030.*

---

## Penalty Structure for Non-Compliance

- **Excess emissions premium:** €95 per g/km of CO2 above target, per vehicle registered in that year
- **Example:** A manufacturer selling 1M cars per year and missing the target by 5 g/km faces a **€475M penalty per year**
- This creates a powerful financial incentive for manufacturers to accelerate EV sales or purchase "credits" from compliant manufacturers (pooling)

## EU Alternative Fuels Infrastructure Regulation (AFIR) — 2023

**Regulation (EU) 2023/1804**

Sets mandatory minimum public charging deployment targets:

| Target | Requirement |
|--------|------------|
| Fast charging (150 kW+) | Every 60 km on TEN-T core network by 2025 |
| Public charging power | 1 kW per BEV registered nationally by 2025 |
| Urban charging pools | Major urban nodes covered by 2025 |
| EU-wide public chargers (2030 target) | ~3.5 million units |

## Euro 7 Emissions Standard

**Regulation (EU) 2024/1257** — Applies to new type approvals from:
- July 2025 (passenger cars and light commercial vehicles)

Increases compliance costs for ICE vehicles (tighter NOx, particulate limits + durability requirements), narrowing the cost-of-compliance gap relative to BEVs.
